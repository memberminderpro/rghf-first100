UNDERSTANDING THE PROJECT: "Rotary Global History"

One of the early challenges facing Rotary was the "translation" from Paul Harris' idea in Chicago to other cultures and languages.

 

Now, Rotary Global History is following the same path, or rather a plan, to bring the history of Rotary to all languages of Rotary. The task will take years and many volunteers from the "Language Communities" of Rotary.

 

Jack M. B. Selway

 

In Chapter 4, page 87 of "This Rotarian Age" (1935) founder Paul Harris writes of the skepticism some had that Rotary could even make the leap to the social structure of London. Today, Rotary in England is very strong and became so from the outset.

 

On page 89, he writes: "A Chicago member, who was about to make a business visit to Cuba, was prevailed upon to attempt the organization of a club in Havana. He did his best, but his efforts were doomed to failure. In a spirit of hopefulness he had undertaken his mission; in a spirit of despair he returned, contending that Rotary was solely an Anglo-Saxon idea; that it never could be appreciated or understood by other races. Those who have been privileged to become acquainted with the splendid Latin American Rotarians know how erroneous were his conclusions."

 

In early 2003, clubs from District 4150 in Mexico began our first project to translate the entire history into Spanish. This work was created by Carlos Garcia Calzada, then of RC Puerto Vallarta Centro, Mexico.

Visitors may also find that many browsers and internet organizations provide some translation of web pages
