RGHF MISSION

A rare photo display

of the professional work environment

of the founder of Rotary

Harris writes in "The Founder of Rotary" about his first partnership. Another section describes another long time partner.

 

The outer office of Harris Dodds & Brown

The interior of the outer office

Mr. Ragy's Office, most likely an associate clerk in the firm

Mr. Marshal's office. Another associate or clerk (note clerk with cigar in his mouth)

Mr. Brown's office. A partner in the firm. Sparse decorations.

Mr. Dodd's office. A partner in the firm to whom Paul would turn for support when he left the practice of law around 1930.

A view of Paul Harris' office. Note the decorations, including a "Tiffany" type lamp.

Mr. Harris' private office. The law office of Paul Harris until about 1930 when he left the practice of law.

Note the spittoon and large library

Portrait of Harris with secretary. Date unknown.

A 1942 Photo of the "Harris Gallery" prior to moving to the new RI Headquarters.

 

Photographs on this page are from the archives of the Rotary Club of Wallingford Vermont, the home town of Paul Harris and are part of an archival work by Matts O. Ingemanson.
