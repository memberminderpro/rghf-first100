Rotary's Second Batch of Women District Governors
 
Zone 32, District 7890, Carolyn Pierce, Dalton, MA
 
Carolyn W. Pierce, District 7890

Joined Rotary in December. 1988

Club Secretary in 1989-90

Became President of the Dalton Rotary Club in 1990-91

Club Level

Served, at various times, as the chair of all Four Avenues of Service, served on the club's Board of directors six terms, headed the club's largest fundraiser, "The Dalton Rotary Follies" from 1986 (before becoming a Rotarian) until 1992. (This project usually nets between $10,000 and $12,000), served as membership chair three times, and started the Dalton Club's Jr. Rotarian program, served 4 times as the Dalton Rotary Club's Foundation Chair, Club Secretary 4 times,

In 2010 I retired from the world of Real Estate and now have time to serve Rotary as a club Rotarian in the Tri-Town Rotary club which I joined in 2006.

District Level:

Governor's Representative 3 years

District Governor for the year 1996-97

Served as Chair of the Interact Rotaract Committee 2 years:

A member of the Districts' RYLA committee for four years.

District 7890 Foundation Chair 1997-99, 2001-2002, 2002-2003

Chair for Annual giving for 1999-2000

District Permanent Fund Chair 2003-2004

District Trainer, preparing for the years 2000-2001, 2004-2005,2005-2006, 2007-2008, 2009-2010

ZONE ACTIVITIES

Chair of the 8 District, Northeast Multi-District PETS in 1997.

Training leader for the Multi-District PETS in 1998, 1999, 2001, 2003, 2004

Facilitator at the Multi District Pets 1995-2004

Served as Governor Nominee Coordinator for the Institute in Bethesda.

Division Head for Promotions for the Zone 31 and 32 Rotary Institute in Montreal", and again Promotions Chair for the Zone Institute in 1999 in Newport R.I.

Served as a faculty member for the Leadership Forum in Newport, devising a program of training for the Governors 2001-2002

Chair of the Leadership Forum in Williamsburg for 2003

Member of the original committee for Northeast LINK program for Ambassadorial Scholars In charge of training facilitators, and the training of VIP Aides,

Key Note Speaker 2002, 2004, 2008

Received a 2 two year appointment as a Foundation Group Leader for Zone 31 and as a member of the Foundation Training Committee.

Member of the Zones 31 and 32 Permanent Fund Training Committee.

Served as Chair of the Foundation Training Committee for Zones 31-32 for years 2002-2004. We provide in district specific training upon request

Served as Permanent Fund National Advisor 2003-2004

Appointed

Rotary International Membership Coordinator for the years 2001-2004

Training leader for next class of RIMC's in Evanston


Appointed to the position of Regional Rotary Foundation Coordinator for Zone 31 for 2004-2007

Major Donor and Foundation Benefactor, Multiple Paul Harris Fellow

Recipient of the Citation for Meritorious Service, awarded by the Trustees of the Rotary Foundation, and the Rotary Foundation's highest honor, the

Distinguished Service Award

Have represented 8 Rotary International Presidents as their Representative at District Conferences around the United States

COMMUNITY INVOLVEMENT

I am active in my church, serving as a Vestry member for Grace Episcopal Church, and also as Jr. Warden ( vice chair) of that body. I served a second term as the Senior Warden ( Chair) in Jan of 2000 I recently chaired a successful Capital Fund Drive for the church

In 1998-99 I was the Chair of the Realtor Community Service Committee, and am on the membership task force for the Berkshire Chamber of Commerce.

For many years I led the fund raising committee of one our local hospitals, producing and directing musical variety shows.

PERSONAL

I have three grown children, Lauren, Keith and Jeffery, and 4 grandchildren, and 3 great grand children.
