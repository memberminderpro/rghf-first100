THE DISTRICT 5770 HISTORY

Prepared by the district and not verified by Rotary Global History

Brief History of District 5770

The designation of our Rotary district has changed eight times through the years. What we know now as District 5770 began as District 11 in 1915. The district was extremely large and included Kansas and Oklahoma. As the years went by, the district became smaller as Rotary grew larger. In 1922-1926 the district became District 17. In 1924-1925, the President of Rotary International was Everett W. Hill, a member of the Shawnee, OK Rotary Club. At that time there were 1796 Clubs with 102,000 members in 34 countries, when he took office. His year ended with 2,096 Clubs.

In ensuing years the district was known as District 12 from 1926-1937; District 124 from 1937-1949; District 181 from 1949-1953; District 182 from 1953-1957; District 577 from 1957-1990; and District 5770 from 1990 through the president.

The description of District 5770 that was approved on July 1, 1969 included: That portion west of the western boundaries of the counties of Okfuskee, McIntosh, Pittsburg, Pushmataha and Choctaw, and south of the southern boundaries of the counties of Lincoln, Oklahoma, Canadian, Blaine, Custer, and Roger Mills.   

In 2008, The following counties are listed in District 5770:  Atoka, Beckham, Bryan, Caddo, Carter, Cleveland, Coal, Comanche, Cotton, Garvin, Grady, Greer, Harmon, Hughes, Jackson, Jefferson, Johnston, Kiowa, Love, Marshall, Murray, Pontotoc, Pottawatomie, Stephens, Tillman, Washita. 

Rotarians receiving award recognition from Rotary International include: Rotary Foundation Citation for Meritorious Service—Ron Burton, James Dolman, Winston Higgs, Karl Kobbs, Bill Smith and Roy Troutt; Rotary Foundation Distinguished Service Award-Ron Burton; Service Above Self Award—Ron Burton, Roy Troutt and Bob Usry; International Service Award for a Polio-Free World—Ron Burton.

The district has provided two Rotarians who served as RI Director– Roy Troutt from 1979-1981, and Ron Burton from 1998-2000. Burton continues to serve as Trustee of the Rotary Foundation from 2006-2010. He is the Vice Chair for 2008/2009.  Herbert J. Taylor, author of the Four-Way Test, was the third president of the Pauls Valley Rotary Club.  He later moved to Chicago and was President of Rotary International from 1954-1955.
