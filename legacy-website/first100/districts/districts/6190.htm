District 6190

A Brief History
List of Governors

Awards and Recognitions

On Saturday February 28, 1914, the Rotary Club of Shreveport was chartered
and became the first Rotary club in North Louisiana.

It became a part of District 12 which comprised Texas and Louisiana with 19 clubs.

     Here are the changes which created District 6190.

1917 - 1918 District 20 comprising Mississippi and Louisiana with 6 clubs.
1918 - 1923  District 14 comprising Northern Mississippi and Louisiana.  Memphis was added in 1921 making 23 clubs.
1923 - 1937 District 17 with the same territory except for Memphis, growing to 63 clubs.
1937 - 1949 District 139 comprising Western Mississippi and Louisiana; 49 clubs
1949 - 1956 District 202 created; territory remains the same; 53 clubs.
1956 - 1957 District 619; territory remains the same; 57 clubs
1957 - 1991  District 619 split into two parts and now comprises North and Central Louisiana with 30 clubs.
1991 Renumbering of all districts by Rotary International. District 619 becomes 6190.
1993 - 1994  Two new clubs chartered.  One established; 31 clubs.
1999 - 2000 One new club chartered.  32 clubs.
2001 - 2002  One new club chartered. 33 clubs.
