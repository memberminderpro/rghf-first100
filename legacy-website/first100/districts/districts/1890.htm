THE HISTORY OF DISTRICT 1890, Germany
A Part of Our History of Rotary in Europe

Prepared by the district and not verified by Rotary Global History

District 1890 is the northernmost district in Germany.

It includes the provinces of Schleswig-Holstein and Hamburg and the northern part of Lower Saxony.

 

Here, 66 Rotary clubs are grouped in our district which support a total of approximately 3,350 active members.

 

The Rotary Club of Hamburg is a member-club of this district. In 1927, RC Hamburg was the first Rotary Club to be formed in Germany and is the ancestor of all German Rotary clubs to our northern neighbors in Denmark.

 

Further details on the German-Danish countries Committee can be found on the District's web site.

 

Like all districts in Germany, we began our life in 1928 as District 73.

All Rotary activity was abandoned between 1938 and 1945 but was not resurrected until 1949 when District 74 was created to again, cover the whole of Gwermany.

 

In 1957 the district number was changed to District 149 which continued to be the only district for our entire country.

It was the early 70s that saw the greatest growth of Rotary in our region and forced the subdivision of district 149 into several other districts. The previous numbering system was also outgrown due to the new clubs and districts around the world.

Old numbers had to be allocated to other countries.

From the "140" numbers, Germany was allocated several "180" numbers while our old 140 numbers remained with Denmark.

 

The present District 1890 was District 189 until 1991 and before that, District 184.

 

We are awaiting additional historical information from the district.
