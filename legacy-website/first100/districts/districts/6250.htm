ROTARY IN WISCONSIN 

In 1913 there were only 83 Rotary Clubs in the world, all serviced from a very small office in Chicago.  Prior to 1915 there were no districts in Rotary; but districting was first instituted in the fiscal year that began July 1, 1915. 

Wisconsin’s first district, which consisted of all of Wisconsin, Minnesota, and the upper Michigan Peninsula, was Number 9.  District 9 lasted three years.  Next was Number 15 which lasted four years.  Then came District 10, which covered four years in the south half of the state, but several years longer in the upper half.  Then District 13 for thirteen years; then District 144 for eleven years in the south half and 143 in the upper half. 

Next came District 210 for seven years, with 208 in the upper half.  Then 210 was divided into 209 in the west half, 210 the east half, and 208 in the upper part of the state for a year or so, and finally 625 in the west, 627 in the east, and 622 up north, with 9 clubs transferred from 208 to 625, and 3 from 208 to 627.  Redistricting then settled down until the 1991/1992 Rotary year, when a cipher was added to district numbers, converting 622, 625, and 627 to 6220, 6250, and 6270. 

The first Rotary club chartered in Wisconsin was in April, 1912, at Superior.  Designated as the 40th club to enter the Rotary world, it was sponsored by the Duluth Club.  June 6, 1913 saw the chartering of the Milwaukee Club (No. 57) and the Madison Club (No. 71).  From that time on, additional clubs have been chartered in all Wisconsin districts, and there are presently 60 clubs in District 6250 alone with the most recent being chartered in the 2006/2007 Rotary year.
