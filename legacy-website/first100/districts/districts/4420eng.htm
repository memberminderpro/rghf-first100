HistORY OF DISTRICT 4420

 

1922 witnessed the foundation of the first Club in Brazil - Rio De Janeiro.

Rotary had 41 districts, encompassing U.K., the United States, Canada, Mexico and Cuba.

 This situation lasted up to 1927, when District 63 was created, enclosing Argentina, Brazil, Uruguay and later Paraguay.
The first Governor was Don Donato Gamiara of Uruguay and as the Cupertino Del Field of Argentina.

The precursory meeting of the District Assemblies of District 63 in Saints, confirmed –

(a) that ”Rotaryano” (with Y) could be used only for internal correspondence;
(b) the creation of a magazine, embryo of current Brazil Rotário.
(c) support for the next governor to be from Argentina, despite a Brazilian District included, and demonstrating our love and respect for our neighbouring country.
In the same year, the first convention of Rotary Clubs in Brazil was held to include Rio De Janeiro, São Paulo and Santos,

Juiz De Fora and Belo Horizonte, governed until until 8 December in “The River”. The District Committee’s visit to the President of Brazil, Washington Luiz, was published in the Official Gazette.
Subjects discussed included the postal service, ethical advertising, beggary, combating illiteracy, protection to the child, recovery of alcoholics, re-education of mutilated.
In commemoration of the 50th anniversary of the Republic of Brazil, each Brazilian Rotary Club donated funds for a building destined for a primary school and called “Rotary School”, to be inaugurated by 15 November 1939. Moreover, our District campaigned to make obligatory, primary education in Brazil.

It was also at this Convention that the decision to require all Rotary meetings to begin with a greeting of the flag. On 26 July 1929, the RC of Rio De Janeiro was the first Club to adopt this practice.

1928 April – 1st Convention of District 63 in Buenos Aires.
1929 April and May – 2nd Conference of District 63. The creation of District 72 to include Rio De Janeiro; Edmundo de Carvalho, at the time President of RC São Paulo was elected District Governor. There were 12 clubs.

1930 – 2/3 May – 1st District Conference in São Paulo.
1931/32 - Samuel Leão de Moura (18 clubs) of RC Saints, one of the great names in Brazilian Rotary.

1935/36 - Election of Arruda Pereira of RC São Paulo. He was the first Brazilian to exert the Presidency.
1939 - District 72 was divided into 4 new Districts – 26, 27, 28, and 29.
The state of São Paulo was allocated District 28 to include Mato Grosso, part of Mines and São Paulo, and the cities of Wells of Caldas, Araraquara and Mogi of Crosses.
This division caused great controversy and dissatisfaction, and almost destroyed Rotary activity to function in this division.

1950 – District 119 was created by the renumbering of these divisions along with Districts 41, 43 and 72. This was the first of several attempts to solve the conflict.

1952-53 – District 122 was ceated for Mato Grosso and part of the State of São Paulo in a confused description of latitudes and longitudes.

1953 - District 140 created and District 122 is more clearly defined - Mato Grosso and São Paulo to the south of the river Tietê, the northwest of a line straight line binding the bar of the River Piracicaba to the Tietê, north of the River Fish.

1958 – Districts 119 renumbered to District 461.

1959 - District 462 and District 459 were partitioned from District 461.

All these controversial divisions, redistributions and renumbering were too much for many of the original members. The loyalty to, and spirit of, the old District 72 was so strong that children, grandsons and great-grandsons congregated each 2 years provoking nostalgia and “homesickness under the nickname of “The Widowers”. This same spirit occurred  in District 461.

Finally, in 1989 to the credit of our Governor and the support of Nelson Célico,  the idea was to take the Square of the Sé as the reference point, and from there to take off 3 districts in a radial direction,

With the support of the College of Governors and 100% of the clubs and thanks to the work of engineer Carlos Eugênio Prézia of RC SP Garden of the Flags, the new map was created.

Thus, Nelson Aparecido Célico was the last Governor of District 461.

Finally, District 4420 was created for the south, from Lower Santista; District 4430 for the north-east as far as Mogi of Crosses; and District 4610 the west including Osasco.
The first Governor of District 4420 was Flávio Farah (1991-92) of RC SP - Interlagos.


This final redistribution has provoked greater multiplication of Rotary movements and stronger loyalties since the days of “The Widowers”.

Adaptation of the story by the PDG Hugo Maia de Arruda Pereira (1989/90 - District. 4610 - RC of São Paulo).
