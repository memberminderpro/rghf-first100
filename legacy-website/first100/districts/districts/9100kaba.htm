PP. GASTON KABA, SAS
This brief biographical summary refers only to matters pertinent to activities as a Rotarian.

PP Gaston Kaba was born in Dakar, Senegal, West Africa on 25 November 1941.

He took higher education at universities in Leeds and London in England and in New York and Los Angeles in America.
Gaston has been heavily involved in Peace Corps activities and with the USAID program throughout Niger with occasional periods in Canada and America.
Gaston was also appointed as Secretary General in the Ministry of Higher education in the Senegal government.

His involvement in Africare eventually lead him towards Rotary where he acted as the national chairman of the Polio Plus Committee covering all clubs in Niamey, Niger between 1988 and 2008.
Between 1988 and 2000, Gaston was a member of the Rotary Club of Niamey and president for the 1993/94 year.
Between 1993 and 2003 he was a member of the African Regional Polio Plus Committee.
He was the founder of the Rotary Club Niamey-Croix-du-Sud, a member from 2001 to date, and its president for the 2006/07 year.
He remains a member of the Inter Agency Coordinating Committee (ICC) on Immunization in Niger.
On behalf of District 5100 in Oregon, Canada, he acted as a Niger consultant for a Project Fair titled "Partners in Service" covering 12 clubs throughout Niger.
Similarly, on behalf of District 9100, he assisted in the presentation of a “School Furniture Equipment Education Project” at the “Foire aux Projets de l’Afrique de l’Ouest” (West Africa Project Fair) in Abidjan, Cote d’Ivoire.

He was mandated by the President of Rotary International to conduct several missions in:
Nigeria, Kenya, Ivory Coast, Togo, Ghana, Uganda, Cameroon, Zimbabwe, Senegal, Chad, Nigeria, Burkina Faso, Benin, Ethiopia, South Africa, to discuss and indulge in activities pertaining to the PolioPlus Eradication Programs in the Western, Central and Southern African Epidemiological Blocks, including Niger.

Since 1988 he has been taking an active role in the initiative of eradicating polio since the inception of the Expanded Program on Immunization (Programme Elargi de Vaccination, PEV-Niger) and since the inception ofNational Immunization Days (NIDs) and Local Immunization days (LIDs) against polio from 1997 to this day.

He has received various awards from District 9100 Governors for his work on polio eradication and in 2007 received the "Award for a Polio Free World" from Rotary International.
Also in 2007, he was honoured with the "Service Above Self" award.

The Peace Corp and Rotary International have been working together on many projects over the years and through his involvement as Associate Peace Corps Director, he has attended many workshops and conferences on health and HIV issues in Mauritania, Washington DC, Mali, in Abidjan Cote d’Ivoire, in Ouagadougou Burkina Faso, Zambia and Cotonou.
