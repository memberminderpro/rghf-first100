THE HISTORY OF DISTRICT 2240, Czech Republic and Slovak Republic

A Part of Our History of Rotary in Europe

Prepared by the district and not verified by Rotary Global History

ROTARY TRADITION IN THE FORMER CZECHOSLOVAKIA UP TO 1938
The first club in the former Czechoslavakia was the Rotary Club of Prague founded in 1925 which was followed within a few years by the Rotary Club of Pilsen founded in 1927.

District 66 was established in 1927 with 47 active Clubs in 1938, the 3rd biggest District in Europe

SERIOUS TIMES . . .
1938-1945 – Nazism banned Rotary in Czechoslovakia
1946-1948 – short period of Rotary restoration after the WW2
1948-1989 – complete abolishment of Rotary by the Communists

NEW FREE TIMES AFTER THE VELVET REVOLUTION 1989 
Re-establishment of the former Rotary Clubs and founding of new Clubs in the framework of District 1920 (Austria-West) together with great hospitality, friendship and support from different Rotary Clubs worldwide, quick incorporation and acceptance into the Rotary family

In 1999, the independent international Rotary District 2240 inaugurated officially.
District 2240 now includes 17 very active Rotary Clubs in the Slovak Republic and 43 very active Rotary Clubs in the Czech Republic
There are also 11 Rotaract Clubs overall and it grows steadily

The last but not the least one RC Telč chartered on May, 6, 2006.
