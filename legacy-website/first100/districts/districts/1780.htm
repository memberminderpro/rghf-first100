THE HISTORY OF DISTRICT 1780, France
A Part of Our History of Rotary in Europe

Also, the start of Rotary in France



Prepared by the district and not verified by Rotary Global History

In 1905, there were in the world, one district and one club.
After 99 years, we expect 31,314 clubs that cover the entire planet with 1,220,543 Rotarians and 530 districts.
(figures at 31/03/2004)

District 1780 covers the area of France in the regions of Rhone-Alps - Mont Blanc.

In 1921, during the implantation of Rotary in our country, was created DISTRICT 49, then the District 48 with Maurice ARGOD from RC Romans as Governor.

It corresponded to the current region of the Rhone-Alps.
Until 1992, we stayed in District 171 covering 8 departments: the AIN - the ARDÈCHE - the DROME - iSeries - the LOIRE - the RHONE - the SAVOIE - Haute Savoie

In the late 1980s, when the district covering all of the Rhone-Alpes region was becoming difficult to manage with its 3,000 Rotarians and its 66 clubs, the decision was taken to cut the DISTRICT 171 into two parts corresponding to the Lyon Academy (by becoming the DISTRICT 1710) and the Academy of GRENOBLE (by becoming the DISTRICT 1780).

District 1780 is 5 departments:

  • - l'ARDÈCHE - the DROME -l'ISÈRE(except the districts of Vienne Nod, Vienne Sud, Roussillon, Beaurepaire) - the SAVOIE - the HAUTE SAVOIE
  • - 48 Rotary Clubs,
  • - 1679 Rotarians,
  • - 23 former governors.

Our DISTRICT 1780, has a young history since it was established in 1992 under the governorate of Jean BERTHON of the Rotary Club of Montelimar.

Since 1992, our District is growing slowly, with ups and downs, the number of Rotarians rising from 1,550, to 1679 today.
The number of clubs have also risen from 35 to 48.
