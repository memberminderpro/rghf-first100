GEOFFREY B.W. LITTLE

Return to D9680

aka THE SMILING POLICEMAN

PP Geoffrey B. W. Little, J.P., PHF

  • Member RC North Sydney Sunrise District 9680
  • Founding International Chairman and C.E.O Police Law Enforcement Professionals Fellowship of Rotarians
  • Special Ambassador and Dean of the Ambassadorial Chair - Rotary District (9700) Peace Communities Project
  • Honorary Member: RC Wagga Wagga - Kooringal, D9700
  • Honorary Member: RC Makati - Edsa Manila Philippines D3810
  • Honorary Member: RC Zamboanga City Philippines D3850

Sergeant Geoffrey Bernard William Little retired from the New South Wales Police Service in 2001.  Little joined the New South Wales (Australia) Police Cadet Corps on August 13, 1962. He was sworn in as a Probationary Constable on his birthday, June 8, 1965 and was promoted to `Sergeant Third Class' on Valentine's Day, February 14, 1982.  He says; "Somebody loved me!".

Geoffrey retired at the equivalent Rank of Second Class/Sergeant/Staff Sergeant/Incremental Sergeant  after nearly 40 years with the New South Wales Police.  He is sixty something years young, long time divorced, has a grown daughter and son - AND a couple of mortgages.

Little has been recognised Internationally for his contribution to `Community Based Policing' and is an Honorary Deputy Sheriff-Lieutenant, Ozaukee County Sheriff's Department, Wisconsin, U.S.A.; Honorary Sergeant, Northern Constabulary, Inverness, Scotland; Honorary Lieutenant Colonel (Superintendent), Philippine National Police (PNP) attached to the Office of the Presidential Consultant on Police Affairs, Presidential Security Group, Malacanang Palace, Manila, Philippines; and Honorary Deputy Chief, Indianapolis Metropolitan Police Department, Indiana, USA.

Sgt Geoffrey Little was dubbed The Smiling Policeman by the press in 1977 when his boss busted him for smiling too much during traffic duties.

Yesterday, a strange twist of fate brought the policeman face to face with his past. A portrait painted of him for the 1977 Archibald Prize by artist Phillip Cooper was found at Nowra Waste Dept on Monday, destined for landfill.

"I was sitting here in my office and got a phone call" enthused Sergeant Little. "The boys from Nowra were coming up to pick up some glocks, some new pistols, and the painting was in the back of the car."

Mr. Steve Foord, a retired ranger with the Shoalhaven Council and retired Special Constable with the NSW Police, was at the tip on the South Coast when he saw the painting.

"I was having a little look around when I saw it," he said. "The artist was throwing out these paintings he had. There were probably up to 200 paintings in the pile."

"I just noticed the portrait with the police badge number and I thought obviously the Police Department would be able to trace who it was. I thought it was a very nice painting"

Sergeant Little, now at North Sydney, made headlines in 1977 when he was told by a senior officer to stop being so happy. The then constable would direct traffic on the corner of Bent and Young Streets for two mornings a week, before choir practice. "He told me I had a problem" recalled Little.

He said, "Geoffrey, you go around loving people and smiling too much. You should be more like a policeman."

Sergeant Little has never shied away from the profile created by the furor. He has appeared on Romper Room and traveled the world promoting smiles as a United Nations Ambassador of Goodwill. There have even been media events in America, where he has directed traffic for the local constabulary.

Argyle Centre artist, Phillip Cooper asked him to sit for an Archibald portrait. The painting never made it to the finals, but was hung at The Rocks Mall for about 5 years.

"I used to go back quite a lot to see how it was going but then Phillip moved to Nowra," Sergeant Little said.

"He asked me if I would like to buy it and he put a price tag on it of about $300. In those days I didn't have $300 so I had to sadly wave goodbye to a part of me."

HIS ACHIEVEMENTS

Apart from his founding the "PoLPFR", Little has also been the driving force for these additional world-wide activities -

ROTARY DISTRICT PEACE COMMUNITIES PROGRAMME (RDPCP)

The Rotary District Peace Commmunities Programme (RDPCP) has been a principal programme of Polepfor.  The RDPCP has undergone quite an evolution since the Project was first posted as the Rotary Peace Project.  After Little attended the Rotary Peace Summit in Windsor, Ontario, April 2008, this program has progressed far insofar as there is now a Proposal (Memorial) to the Council of Legislation (CoL) to request the RDPCP become a Rotary International based exercise.  The Programme started in 1992 as a Rotary Club initiative out of the RC Wagga Wagga - Kooringal D9700, then it became a Rotary District Peace Communities Programme - and subsequently, began its enormous spread around the world, mostly due to Little's perserverence, initiatives and personal contacts.

This is the programme with which Little has developed his most ardent love affair and which, since his retirement, he has devoted his time with the most extraordinary results.

 

THE EVILS OF DRUG ABUSE AND ILLEGAL DRUG TRAFFICKING

The world-wide offensive being waged by Police and Law Enforcement Agencies against illegal drug trafficking, drug abuse and the delivery of `Drug Abuse Prevention' lectures and seminars to the youth of the world, is a matter all Rotarians and members of Police and Law Enforcement should be addressing. Prevalent in Cebu at the time was a bout of Rugby sniffing (shoe glue), Cough Syrup abuse, Shabu (Marijuana) and the `poor man's cocaine - ice, which has now become the scourge of the drug scene here in Sydney.

As Rotarians, the PoLEFoR is anxious to promote the involvement of the proud "Vocation of Police and Law Enforcement' in the work of Rotary, and conversely, encourage those who might not be Rotarians, to seriously consider Rotary involvement within their community.

The 'Police/Law Enforcement Professionals Fellowship of Rotarians' looks at assisting and complementing your own Rotary Service and helping to promote and enhance your own personal Rotary programmes.

Little engaged in one such activity in Cebu, Philippines, an exercise conducted through the Cebu City Office of Substance Abuse Prevention (COSAP).  This and many other programmes are available for a Rotary input wherever Rotarians may serve and of whichever Rotary Club you are a Member.

"JUST SAY NO" TO DRUGS PROGRAMME, CEBU CITY, PHILIPPINES

One such activity has been the 'Just Say No' to Drugs' Programme in Cebu, Philippines, formerly under the Chairmanship of Rotarian (former Philippine Constabulary) Oscar C. Mabilog, Rotary Club of Metro Cebu. In his constant battle to promote this programme, Little was instrumental in creating the programme.

The now Late Rotarian Oscar Mabilog was an Honorary Deputy Mayor of Cebu City and reigned for a number of years as the Chairman of COSAP (Cebu City Office of Substance Abuse Prevention)
