HISTORY OF DISTRICT 4410

The history of district 4410 traces the birth of Rotary in the south eastern state of Espirito Santo (ES). Rotary however, has already existed in Brazil for 11 years.

On 25 August 1934, after some preliminary meetings, Rotary came to the ES via Mr. James Roth, emissary of the Rotary International.

On 29 August 1934, the first official meeting of the Rotary Club de Vitória was held with the following committee, who would hold post until 30 June 1935 –

President - Alberto de Oliveira Santos,

1st Vice-president - Nicholas Von Schilgen,

2nd Vice-president - Joshua the Prado,

Secretary - Moacir Barbosa Soares,

Bursar - Jaime Coelho de Almeida,

Director of Protocol - Jones Dos Santos Neves,

Committee - Osvaldo Cruz Guimarães, Pietrângelo De Biase and Aurino Quintaes.

Rotary in Espirito Santo grew in 1941 with the formations of Rotary Clubs Colatina and Guaçuí.

 In 1944, Aurino Quintaes was the first person chosen from the State for District 27 Governor. District 27 included the States of Rio De Janeiro, Part of Minas Gerais and the State of the Espirito Santo.

 In 1949/50, another District Governor is chosen from ES which, under redistribution and now called District 118, now includes Espirito Santo, Mines and Rio De Janeiro.

In 1959 RC Victory-West is established.

Another redistribution passes ES to District 458 that includes part of Rio De Janeiro and the bush area of Minas Gerais.

 For Rotary year 1960/61, Manoel Brave Maria Ramos, of the RC Victory, was appointed District Governor.

Other Clubs now included Conceição of the Bar (1968), Colatina Is Silvano (1969), Old Village (1969), Linhares (1970), Long Victory-Beach (1970) and Guarapari (1970).

Other District Governors included Nicanor Paiva of RC Victory (1972/73), and Elviro de Freitas of RC Cachoeiro de Itapemirim 1975/76  RC Cachoeiro-west was formed in 1977.

In 1978/79 the District Governor was Jose Palm of RC Victory and new clubs are formed RC Mateus, RC Old-Beach Village, R.C. Victory-Beach de Camburi.

In 1982/83 District Governor Robert Viana of RC Victory-West was appointed.

New clubs included RC New Colatina-Brazil, RC Sundays Martins, RC Mountain Range. Necklaces Vieira of RC Long Victory-Beach bacame Governor for period 1984/85. More new Clubs formed - RC Muqui, RC Peter Canarian, RC River Banana Plantation.

 In 1988/89 Teixeira Sobreira of the RC Victory, is the last person from Espirito Santo to manage District 458.

More Clubs are created - RC Great Cariacica-field, RC Village Old-Beach de Itapoã, RC Jaguaré, RC Iconha, RC Glad, RC Gabriel of the Straw, RC Victory-Side Sea, RC Jucutuquara Victory.

District 458 now covers much of the states of Mines and Espirito Santo reaching in 1991, a total of 72 clubs.

 In 1992 the difficulty of the governors to visit all clubs caused a final redistribution, when District 4410 was created solely to cover the clubs of Espirito Santo, while district 458 was transformed into D4580.

The first governor of this new District was Geraldo Gonzaga of RC Victory, who died before finishing his mandate. His 1992/93 term was concluded by Teixeira Sobreira.

 From there the District grew with the formation of RC America Cariacica-Garden, RC New River in the south, RC Marataízes, RC Victory-Beach of Canto, and RC Mountain Range.

The District was then governed by Luiz Antonio Murad (1993/94), Marinho Salviano of the Coast (1994/95), Sergio Ottoni Byaardt (1995/96), Ivan Belfort Shalders (1996/97) and Luiz Alberto Tavares (1997/98).

Much of the State of the Espirito Santo especially the northern region, experienced serious drought although the 35 clubs that comprise the District, keep firm the ideals of Rotary International.

The district walks into a promising future converting the dreams of Rotary into reality.
