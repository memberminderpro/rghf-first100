HistORY OF DISTRICT 4470 in Brazil and Paraguay

 

A Part of Our History of Rotary in South America

 

Prepared by the district and not verified by Rotary Global History

Until 1927, the existing clubs outside U.K., America, Mexico and Cuba had not been allocated District numbers. in Brazil, Rio De Janeiro and São Paulo already existed. In this year, District 63 was created, enclosing Argentina, Uruguay, Brazil (six Rotary Clubs) and later, Paraguay.

In 1929, all 13 Rotary Clubs of Brazil were integrated in District 72 but in 1938, were subdivided into four districts. District 28 covered the States of Goiás and Mato Grosso, and part of the States of São Paulo and Minas Gerais.

In 1949, District 119 arose to incorporate “part of the State of São Paulo (excluding the Valley of the Paraíba and the region of Campinas) and all the State of Mato Grosso”. At this time, Brazil had 7 Districts.

In 1952, with 8 Districts, ours became District 122, with the limits understood for the “State of Mato Grosso, that part of the State of São Paulo to the north of the River Fish, until latitude 22o30', latitude 22o30' between the longitudes 50o and 49, latitude 22o 30' between the longitudes 49 and 47o37' the west of the longitude 47o37' between the latitudes 22o50' and 22o20', south of the latitude 22o20' until the longitude 48o30', the west of the longitude of 48o30' until the River Disturbance, and south of the River Disturbance and the Great River until the junction of this with Rio Paranaíba”.

In 1957, we are District 451, with 31 clubs, and the limits of “State of the Mato Grosso (at that time one alone State was) and part of the State of São Paulo enter the south of Rio Tietê, north of the River of the Fish, and broken line of the River of the Fish, latitude 50o to the bar of the Rivers Clearly and Medium brown and from there to the bar of Rio Tietê and Rio Piracicaba”.

In 1977, we are District 447, with 35 clubs, in the limits “Brazil - Mato Grosso; State of São Paulo, that part limited for Rio Tietê, longitude 50o, and Rio Aguapeí; Paraguay - the City of Peter Juan Caballero”.

By 1989, District 447 reached 93 clubs, forcing another  redistribution, separating the current State of Mato Grosso, to constitute District 444. Our District 447, later adopts our present number 4470, with the limits of “BRAZIL - Been of Mato Grosso of the South; State of São Paulo, that part bordered by the estuary of Rio Tietê with the River Paraná, until the longitude 50o, and Rio Aguapeí, until its estuary in the River Paraná; PARAGUAY - the City of Peter Juan Caballero” (Today, the City of Capitan Bado, in Paraguay, belongs to our district; established in Rotary year 1988-89).

Of the Rotary Clubs that comprise our District, the oldest is the RC of Great Field (#68 of Brazil), organized in 15 Dec 1939 and admitted on the 15 Jan 1940, having as godfather the RC of Bauru-SP. On 2 Jan 1940, the RC of Corumbá-MS was established (#69 of Brazil), and admitted in 29 Jan 1940.

The first Clubs in the State of São Paulo, were the RC of Araçatuba (#134 of Brazil), admitted 19 Jun 1944), and the RC of Valparaiso (#159 of Brazil), admitted in 6 Nov 1946.

Until the year of 1947, conferences were District Conferences of all RCs of Brazil. In 1948, District 28 held its own independant Conference in São Paulo, but in 1949, the Brazilian districts were for the last time, reunified at one conference, being known as the “Conference of the Farewell”.

In 1965, District 457 hosted a National Conference in Rio De Janeiro.

The first International Convention was held in Brazil Rio De Janeiro in 1948 with 7511 participants. The second International Convention was held in São Paulo in 1981 with 18309 participants.

In 1940/41, Civil engineer, Seting of Arruda Pereira, of the RC of São Paulo, then of District 28, became the first Brazilian to be elected President of Rotary International..

In 1965-66, the lawyer Ernesto Imbassahy de Mello, of the RC Niterói became the second President of Rotary International whole our third Rotary International President was the architect Pablo Viriato Corrêa of the Coast, of the RC Santos (SP), in 1990-91.

In 1951, the RC Porã Tip was established. In 1972, it changed its name to RC Porã-MS Tip, and representing members from Brazil and Paraguay, became the first bi-national club of Brazil.

Later, in 1982-83, the RC Porã-MS-Brazil Tip/Juan Caballero-AM-Paraguay-Border, was established and in 1988/89, the third bi-national club, the RC of Colonel Sapucaia-MS-Brasil/Capitan Bado-AM-Paraguay. Only 6 bi-national clubs exist in the world and 3 of those bi-national clubs exist in District 4470.

Currently, there are 69 clubs in District 4470 with 1704 members of which 8% are women.
