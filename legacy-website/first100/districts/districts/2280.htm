THE HISTORY OF DISTRICT 2280 - Norway

 

A Part of Our History of Rotary in Europe

 

Prepared by the district and not verified by Rotary Global History

The history of the Governor's chain dates back to the first half of the year 1973, after what so far is the discovery of available material.

 

In 1973-74, Guttorm Hareide was Governor for District 128. Previously he had been President of the Istanbul Rotary Club in the year 1967-1968. He was also awarded a PHF.
It was Hareide's idea to create a Governor's Chain for the District.

There is not much information around about how the chain was made but it has been investigated in the old archives and correspondence of the time.
There is not much that is found, but the work on this continues.

However, the following has been unearthed from all those many old documents that have been found so far -
July 1973 - confirmed in a communication from the Bergen company, Theodor Olsens Eftf, that they had received drawings of the district chain.


The company was sorry that "because of their visitors it will take some time to complete."
In a letter of 4 August of the same year, further references to the work on the necklace is made.

In a letter dated 17 October 1973 to the same company in Bergen, Guttorm Hareide sent his thanks "a little late", for the excellent work performed on the necklace of District 128.

It will be on this basis that we assumed that the necklace was completed before October 1973.
In the same letter he asked for an estimate of its value for insurance purposes.


In a document from the jeweler Fridtjof Møller in Trondheim, (he was known to Rotary) that the valuation was performed - because the price of jewelry rose profusely, the necklace was valued at NOK50.000.


The year when this was done is not known because the document where it is clearly mentioned on page 2, is missing page 1, but there are things that suggest that this was done in 1975. See a large image here
 

At the bottom of the chain can be seen discs with "RI" and "District 128".
The disc labeled "67" is the chain's oldest part.
We believe that this refers to the time when Norway was a single district and numbered 67.


On the square between each number was engraved the names of the District Governors, but because of diameter of the chain, the numbers are not in exactly the right range.

 

======================================

 

Rotary began in Norway as District 67. Three districts were allocated to Norway around 1961 with District 128 covering the central region of the country. The present D2280 covers very similar boundaries to that of the earlier D128.

Norway is now divided into 7 districts from 2270 in the far north to 2290 in the south.
