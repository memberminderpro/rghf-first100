THE HISTORY OF DISTRICT 2450

This District covers countries in Europe, Asia and Africa.

Prepared by the district and not verified by Rotary Global History

 
CLUBS
 
Rotary District is totally unique in the World of Rotary and has reason to be proud -
Our Rotary district covers eleven (11) countries in three different continents, speaking different languages, and having different cultures. We are united under the Rotary theme of "Service Above Self". Our aim is to provide service in our countries and to promote peace and mutual understanding between different cultures.
 
Find out about our District social, economic, cultural, environmental, educational, and humanitarian activities, our development plans, our greatest achievements, and our short term and long term objectives. Get to know more about our countries, and taste our different cultures.
 
Our Territory and History
March 11, 1929 witnessed the birth of the Rotary Club of Cairo, Egypt. The first Rotary Club in the Present District, named then "The temporary district of the East of the Mediterranean". Together with the Rotary Clubs of Alexandria (1930), Beirut and Jerusalem (1932), they formed the "District" on July 1st, 1935.
 
Other countries joined our District: Syria (1937 - 1969), Sudan and Cyprus (1938), Jordan (1956) and Bahrain (1965) and Dubai (2001). The district numbering changed several times during the years. It changed from 83 (1935 - 1949), to 89 (1949 - 1970), to 195 (1970 - 1977), to 245 (1977 - 1991) and finally to 2450 since 1991.
 
The district currently includes 150 clubs in 11 countries distributed as shown below.
