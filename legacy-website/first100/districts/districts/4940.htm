ROTARY CLUB ROSARIO “PLAZA”

Its beginnings were around the year 1989, more precisely on January 2, 1989, when at the home of Mr. Armin J. Reutemann, 21 ex-members of Rotary Club Echesortu and former Rotary members of other clubs met to look at the possibility of founding a new club based primarily, not only on the principles and objectives of Rotary, but also on the fundamental idea of forming true friendships and fellowship because it is not possible to form a Rotary Club without the intrinsic, fundamental component of the brotherhood of its members. Some of those members were later founders: Ángel César Abbate, Héctor Sebastián Becchi, Alberto Francisco Bertotto, Ramón E. Cabreras, Hugo Amado Cearras, Ever E. Cimarelli, Hugo Colarte, Elido Lorenzo Cividini, De Vita Eduardo , De Vita Nicolás E, José Floridia, Isidoro J. Portnovy, Armin J. Reutemann, Rubén C. Vincenzi, y, Rolando J. Zanni.

The Club was given the temporary name of Rotary Club Rosario Mariano Moreno (while it was being formed), later, its name was changed to Rotary Club Rosario Plaza, (Act No--23--06/27/89), and finally the current name was chosen as Rotary Club Rosario Plaza de la Bandera (Act No 90--12/11/90).

APPROVAL BY ROTARY INTERNATIONAL

The Charter Letter was received on 01/09/1991 while the President of R.I. was Pablo V.C. Costa; the DG was Roberto Schellhas, and our sponsor was Rotary Club Rosario Río Paraná.

SOME HIGHLIGHTS:

The Club was awarded Presidential Citations in 94-95, 95-96, 96-97, and during the period of 2003/04 and 2007/08, it obtained the Centennial Presidential Mention, in recognition of the projects completed during those years.

Dr. Esteban Laureano Maradona was honored with an Honorary Membership. He was a famous, dedicated physician who inspired with his example by serving his fellow humans. He received numerous meritorious awards which, in a very small way, honored his great humanitarian endeavors. He died on January 14, 1995, at the age of 99. In 1997, an annual competition is held. All medical practitioners are honored regardless of their specialty, and some are selected and awarded citations for meritorious accomplishments in their given professions during the previous year.

Finally, during December 29, 1998, the Municipality of Rosario, honored Dr. Maradona by dedicating a statue in his memory in recognition of his humanitarian work. The statue of Dr. Maradona was inaugurated in the Plaza between Balarce and Rioja streets.

At the present time the Honorary member is Professor Christian Hernández Larguía.

Among the members to the Club, there are three who have received International Exchange Good Will Grants, one leader of IGE, and a Good Will Fellow and International Volunteer.

During the cholera epidemic, the Club donated a state-of-the-art radio equipment to R.C. Tartagal and to the Country Side Hospital to facilitate communications with the Andean Plateau.

The Club has participated in numerous International service projects and one of them was the creation of the only library which includes, exclusively, subjects for the benefit of the handicapped.

The Club is engaged in multiple activities in the Municipality of Rosario, with Father Montaldo, with Sister María Jordán and with the small homes for children and the elderly, etc.

The Club organizes multiple races where several important prizes are offered. The proceeds of these races is used to give donations to many institutions which serve the public good. We also offer forums and presentations through our webpage at: Http//www.RotaryRosarioPlaza.org.ar). On this webpage, the members can access a bank of work projects.

The Club also sponsors, with several participating Rotary members, a food bank that serves the area where the Club is located.

The Club, with the support and the efforts of many members and the support of the Rotary Club of Palm Beach (USA)*has donated to HECA (Emergency Hospital Clemente Álvarez) of the city of Rosario. The donations include a dual-head microscope with foot control and lights in order to do neurological examinations. This high resolution microscope will be used not only for neurological exams, but also to teach physicians who are in the Medical School specializing in brain surgery.

It must be mentioned here that the “Inner Circle” of Rotarians through its many important activities and efforts provide the funds which support many institutions, such as cafeterias for the young as well as for school cafeterias, etc.

On several occasions, the Club has awarded prizes to young people (junior league). Students sponsored by the following professors have received awards: Dr. Carla Ibánez, Prof. Lucas Arrieta, and Dr. María Valeria Lara.

The Rotary Club Rosario “Plaza” has since its origins, been present (through its members) at all events that have been organized by the District Governance and by other clubs in the District.

One must highlight the historical activities of the Fellowship Committee, not only within the Club, but within the whole national territory, including the Argentinean Air Force Base of Marambio in the Antarctic Region of Argentina. On a weekly basis, the Club sends greetings of goodwill to all the members at the base.

Through all these years, the Club has continued to “serve.” Serving is in truth…to give more than we receive in life and of life.

* The Rotary Club of Palm Beach was sponsored by the Rotary West Palm Beach Club. Richard L. Ray provided the inspiration and the motivation for starting another club. During its inauguration, on May 25, 1950, the club had 26 members. The meetings were on Monday and later on Thursdays. Starting in 1951, the meetings were held in Wert´s Restaurant until it was closed in 1973. The building is still in use today, and the restaurant is now Charley´s Crab Restaurant. Since 1973, the meetings have been held at the Breaker´s Hotel.

One of the members is Owen O'Neill, who is also the President of Clinics Can Help (CCH) in the USA which supports health services throughout the world.

In the case of the Rotary Club Plaza de la Bandera, CCH was the organization that provided the medical equipment for the donation mentioned earlier.

ALGUNAS DESTACABLES 

    EL CLUB GANO 3 VECES EL PREMIO  PRESIDENCIAL 94-95  95-96 96-97, Y DURANTE EL PERIODO 2003/4 y 2007/08 OBTUVO LA “MENCION PRESIDENCIAL DEL CENTENARIO”, POR LAS OBRAS REALIZADAS DURANTE DICHO AÑO ROTARIO.- 

    FUE SOCIO HONORARIO EL DR. ESTEBAN LAUREANO MARADONA,   MÉDICO ABNEGADO, QUE  PREDICÓ  CON  EL  EJEMPLO  Y  DEDICÓ  SU VIDA AL SERVICIO DEL PRÓJIMO, POR ELLO RECIBIÓ INNUMERABLES RECONOCIMIENTOS HONORÍFICOS, QUE SOLO MANIFESTARON EN GRADO ÍNFIMO SU LABOR HUMANÍSTICA. FALLECIÓ EL 14 DE ENERO DE 1995, A LOS 99 AÑOS DE EDAD.     COMO UN HOMENAJE A SU MEMORIA NUESTRO CLUB REALIZA A PARTIR DEL AÑO 1997 UN CONCURSO ANUAL DIRIGIDO A TODOS LOS PROFESIONALES DEL ARTE DE CURAR, CUALQUIERA SEA SU ESPECIALIDAD, PARA GALARDONAR A QUIENES HAYAN SOBRESALIDO EL AÑO ANTERIOR EN EL EJERCICIO DE SU PROFESIÓN. 

    FINALMENTE, EL 29 DE DICIEMBRE de 1998 LA MUNICIPALIDAD DE ROSARIO, COMO UN JUSTICIERO HOMENAJE A SU MEMORIA, DEJÓ INAUGURADO UN BUSTO DEL DR. MARADONA EN LA PLAZA SITA EN CALLES BALCARCE Y RIOJA.  

    Y LO ES EN LA ACTUALIDAD, EL MAESTRO DON  CRISTIAN HERNÁNDEZ LARGUÍA, 

    ENTRE SUS MIEMBROS TIENE 3 EX BECARIOS, DE BUENA VOLUNTAD, INTERCAMBISTA INTERNACIONAL, LIDER DE IGE,   ACOMPAÑANTE DE BUENA VOLUNTAD Y VOLUNTARIO INTERNACIONAL.- 

    EN LOS MOMENTOS DE LA EPIDEMIA DE CÓLERA SE DONO UN EQUIPO DE RADIO DE ULTIMA GENERACIÓN AL R. C. TARTAGAL Y A SU VEZ AL HOSPITAL DE CAMPAÑA PARA LAS COMUNICACIONES CON LA PUNA. 

    PARTICIPACIÓN EN INNUMERABLES SERVICIOS INTERNACIONALES, UNO DE ELLOS, LA CREACIÓN DE LA ÚNICA BIBLIOTECA EXCLUSIVA PARA TEMAS DE DISCAPACIDAD. 

    ACTIVIDADES MÚLTIPLES CON MUNICIPALIDAD DE ROSARIO, CON EL PADRE MONTALDO, LA HERMANA MARÍA JORDÁN, LOS HOGARCITOS DE NIÑOS Y ANCIANOS, ETC. 

    ORGANIZAMOS VARIAS CARRERAS DE REGULARIDAD CON IMPORTANTES PREMIOS Y CON EL PRODUCIDO SE HAN EFECTUADO DONACIONES A DISTINTAS INSTITUCIONES DE BIEN PUBLICO,  FOROS, SEMINARIOS, EL CLUB POSEE UNA PAGINA WEB (HTTP://WWW.ROTARYROSARIOPLAZA.ORG.AR) COMO ASIMISMO HA ORGANIZADO UNA BOLSA DE TRABAJO A LA CUAL SE ACCEDE POR LA REFERIDA PAGINA WEB.- 

    TAMBIEN CON LA PRESENCIA DE MIEMBROS DEL CLUB PUDO CONCRETARSE LA CREACIÓN DEL BANCO DE ALIMENTOS, HOY DENTRO DE LA ESFERA DE LA FUNDACION ROTARIA DE ROSARIO;

Y LA DONACION, MERCED AL APOYO DE INUMERABLES  ESFUERZOS DE LOS SOCIOS DEL CLUB Y DEL ROTARY CLUB DE PALM BEACH (EEUU)(*), AL HECA (HOSPITAL DE EMERGENCIAS CLEMENTE ALVAREZ) DE LA CIUDAD DE ROSARIO, CONSISTENTE UN  MICROSCOPIO JK HOPPL DUAL HEAD O-R MICROSCOPE W/DUAL F/O LIGHTS AND X-Y FOOT CONTROL  PARA NEUROLOGIA  

 

 

     ESTE MICROSCOPIO DE ALTA RESOLUCION, NO SOLO SE APLICARA A OPERACIONES SINO TAMBIEN COMO ESCUELA PARA MEDICOS QUE ESTAN REALIZANDO LA ESPECIALIDAD EN CIRUGIA NEUROLOGICA.

     NOTABLE MENCIÓN PARA LA RUEDA INTERNA QUIEN A TRAVÉS DE SUS INTEGRANTES Y CON LA REALIZACIÓN DE MULTIPLES ACTIVIDADES, REÚNE IMPORTANTES FONDOS QUE SE VUELCAN A  INSTITUCIONES TALES COMO COMEDORES INFANTILES, HOGARES ESCUELAS, ETC.- 

 

    HA PRESENTADO EN NUMEROS OCASIONES CANDIDATOS PARA LA OBTENCIÓN DEL PREMIO JOVEN SOBRESALIENTE (CAMARA JUNIOR) HABIENDO OBTENIDO DICHO GALARDON LA DRA. CARLA IBAÑEZ, EL PROF. LUCAS ARRIETA, Y LA  DRA. MARÍA VALERIA LARA 

    EL ROTARY CLUB ROSARIO “PLAZA”, SE HA CARACTERIZADO DESDE SUS ORIGENES POR LA PERMANENTE PRESENCIA DE SUS DISTINTOS FUNCIONARIOS EN CUANTO EVENTO HA SIDO ORGANIZADO POR LAS DISTINTAS GOBERNACIONES DEL DISTRITO, COMO ASIMISMO POR LOS CLUBES DEL MISMO.-  

    Y ES DE DESTACAR LA PRESENCIA HISTORICA DEL COMITÉ DE COMPAÑERISMO, NO SOLO DENTRO DEL CLUB SINO TAMBIEN EN TODO EL TERRITORIO NACIONAL, INCLUSIVE LA BASE MARAMBIO DE LA FUERZA AEREA ARGENTINA, EN EL SECTOR ANTARTICO DE LA REPUBLICA ARGENTINA, HACIENDO LLEGAR, EN FORMA SEMANAL MENSAJES DE SALUTACION Y BUENOS DESEOS.-  

    Y TODOS ESTOS AÑOS HAN  SIDO PUESTOS A "SERVIR"  Y SERVIR ES EN VERDAD...DAR MAS DE LO QUE RECIBIMOS EN LA VIDA Y DE LA VIDA.- 

(*)     El Club Rotary Palm Beach fue generado por el club Rotary West Palm Beach. Richard L. Ray proveyó la inspiración y la motivación para la generación de un nuevo club. En la inauguración, el 25 de Mayo de 1950, contaba con 26 miembros. Las reuniones eran los días Lunes y luego se realizaron los Jueves. Desde 1951 las reuniones se realizaron en el restaurante Wert’s hasta que el mismo cerró en 1973. El edificio todavía existe y es hoy Charley’s Crab Restaurante. Desde 1973 el lugar de reunión ha sido el Hotel Breakers. 

Uno de sus miembros es Owen O'Neill, que a su vez es Presidente de la Clinics Can Help (CCH),  ONG de los Estados Unidos dedicada a la provisión de servicios en el área de salud alrededor del mundo.

Particularmente en el caso de Rotary Club Plaza de la Bandera, CCH ha sido la organización que consiguiera el equipamiento para la donación. referida.-
