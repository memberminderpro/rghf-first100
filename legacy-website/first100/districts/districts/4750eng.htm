HistORY OF DISTRICT 4750

District 4750 was born on 1 July 1985, from the territorial division of old District 457 and enclosed the state of Rio De Janeiro (with exception of the cities that integrated District 460) and whose administration if had become very difficult with its 72 clubs and about 2.600 Rotarians.
The Bay of Guanabara was basically the divider between District 457, with clubs in the city of Rio De Janeiro and the lower end of the State, and District 475 with the north/ northwest of the state.

District 4750 neighbours District 4410 to the north, District 4580 to the northwest, the sTate of Minas Gerais to the south, and District 4570 to the west.

The cities that comprise District 4750 are characterized, in its majority, by its predominantly agricultural activity and cattle, with many poor communities.

District 4750 is comprised of 53 cities, with a Club in at least 31 of them. The District has over 1000 members.

District 4750 is divided into 10 Areas within 5 Regions, each areas being under the responsibility of a Governing Assistant.
