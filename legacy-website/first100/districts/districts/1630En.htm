THE HISTORY OF DISTRICT 1630, Belgium

Prepared by the district and not verified by Rotary Global History

 

District 1630 is one of three districts in Belgium.

 It was not until 1929 to have our own district called District 61.

 District became 68 in 1950, he became District 162 in 1957.

In 1968 the district was divided into 162 and 163 and in 1978 the current division into three districts in 1620, 1630 and 2170 took place in the middle.
Currently it has more than 3,400 Rotarians in 82 clubs.

 The oldest club in our district is the Rotary Club Liege (1926) sponsored by the Rotary club Oostende and Brussels, followed by the Rotary Club Tienen (in 1929) and the Rotary Club Luxembourg - founded in 1929 by the Rotary Club Brussels. Each year new clubs are emerging.
Belgium had the honor of being the district host of the 18th International Convention in 1927 in Oostende. 7000 Rotarians from 40 countries were guests of our Rotarian King, His Majesty the King Albert I, who was also an honorary member of Rc San Fransisco.


The district in 1630 was also the district host the Past Director, Maurice Mathy Rotary Club Liege during the period 1969-1971
