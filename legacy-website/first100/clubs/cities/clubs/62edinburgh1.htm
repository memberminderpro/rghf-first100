THE ROTARY  CLUB  OF  EDINBURGH 

The Edinburgh club was established in 1912

and since then has exerted a great influence

on the Rotary organisation. The first major

contribution was to emphasise the motive

of service so that the commercial aspect

of fellowship could be expanded to embrace

the philosophy of service which remains the

overriding aspect of the Club today. The Club

initiated the building of the Princess Margaret

Rose Hospital for Children in Edinburgh. It has

also helped to start 24 new Rotary clubs in

Scotland and England. In 1991 the Club voted

to accept women members who are now

involved in decision making positions within

the Club The Club has twinned in recent years

with Rotary clubs in Florida, Nice, Fiesole,

Hawaii and helped to found the first Rotary

club in Ukraine, the Rotary Club of Kiev. The

Edinburgh Club is among the biggest in Britain

with close to 150 members from business, the

professions, civil service, academia, consular

service, the clergy and police. 

 

The Rotary Club of Edinburgh is fortunate in

being placed in the heart of the capital city of

Scotland, and as such has access to a very

eclectic variety of speakers covering political,

legal and cultural subjects to local and

international topics. On a weekly basis the Club

is entertained and enlightened as to the challenges

faced by others - such as world record rowing,

political changes in the Far East and The Maine

Lobster Festival. More local topics have included

the Palace of Holyrood House, reports from

ambassadorial scholars and talks by members. 

The Edinburgh Club promotes fellowship through

recreational activities such as the golf club founded

in 1913, curling, flying, sailing, bridge, walking, bowls and petanque.

The Club Community Service is very active and works with numerous Edinburgh Charities. It gives generous support, not only financially but also through the time and effort of the members in week by week attendance as drivers and helpers. There has also been a collaboration with the Salvation Army and Homeless Support groups with the purchase of a minibus for the Cowgate Centre’s work towards integrating the homeless back into society. The Club has also supported the establishment of the Crisis Fare Share food bank with professional advice as well as finances to purchase a computer for

administrative functions of the food network

.

Annually, in June, the Club organises the “Kids Out” Day for some 170 children who have a variety of disabilities ranging from deafness and blindness to mobility and learning difficulties. The Club has close contact with the University of Edinburgh and participate in their Overseas Students Hospitality Scheme.

 

Aqua boxes are also assembled, withdonations

from members, and sent to disaster areas around the world. 

 

One of the attractions of Rotary, particularly in

Edinburgh, is the networking among members

and visitors and due to the location, the welcoming of visitors to the Club is a regular pleasure.
