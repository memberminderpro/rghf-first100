Rotary Club of St. Joseph 32

Rotary International District 6040

 

About St. Joseph 32

105 South 4th St., Suite 304

St. Joseph, M0 64501

Club Information Sheet

 

Rotary is the largest service club in the world.  Rotary is an organization of business and professional leaders united worldwide, who provide humanitarian service, encourage high ethical standards in all vocations, and help build goodwill and peace in the world.  Rotary is some 1.2 million service-minded men and women belonging to more than 28,000 Rotary clubs in virtually every nation in the world.

 

There are three clubs in St. Joseph, East Side, South Side and our Downtown Rotary Club.

 

Our club meetings are held weekly at noon on Tuesdays at the Holiday Inn.  Lunches are always varied and delicious. The buffet line is ready for serving at 11:45 a.m.  A guest speaker is provided weekly by the program committee.  The meeting is from noon to 1:00 p.m.

 

We have about 97 members at the present time.  This may vary a little each year.

 

A fourteen member governing board of directors is selected yearly and serve from July 1 to July l.  They  meet monthly with  an  open invitation to members to attend any of our board meetings during the year.

 

 We plan at least two money making projects yearly.  Our “Travel Vacation” project is started in January with the distribution of tickets to the membership for purchase or selling of ten tickets at $10 each (total $100.)  This project is put in play in January and the winning ticket is drawn at the annual Valentine noon luncheon.

 

                We also participate in the Pumpkinfest held in October.  We have the Pepsi and Coke booths with sign up schedules for our work in the booths.

 

The club is a service club and we contribute to many local organizations yearly as well as international causes: examples – Girl Scouts, Boy Scouts, United Way, Junior Achievement, Allied Arts, Museum, JROTC, to name a few.

 

Scholarship opportunities – The local Rotary Foundation awards five $1000. to seniors here in the St. Joseph High Schools; Ambassadorial scholarships are offered to college students through Rotary International with  one year’s study abroad being paid for by this organization; Rotary District scholarships, usually $1000. are awarded to 10 to 15 senior high school students who reside in District 6040.  Rotary is widely regarded as the world’s largest private provider of international educational scholarships.
