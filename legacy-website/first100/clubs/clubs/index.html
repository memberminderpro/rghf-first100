RGHF MISSION

 

This was the very first of 4,000 pages for RGHF,

created Wednesday 11 October 2000

 

This project began, with this very page, on the club website of Rotary Club of Pueblo, CO, USA #43 on the 11th of October of 2000. Within weeks, the effort became an independent website at www.rotaryfirstfifty.org (represented on this page) and soon was expanded to the "First 100 Clubs" atwww.rotaryfirst100.org . Today this 3,000+ page website is maintained by a dedicated group of Rotary historians. This page is useful for locating any of the "early clubs." Early criticisms of this page were that it was "too long" and "took too long to download." We have since tried to make our project easier to navigate, but some pages, because of the content are still very slow, but worth visiting. The page dealth only with the first 100 clubs and a few other special historically significant clubs. The zone, below, are archival and historic and represent Rotary at the turn of the century. We have other sections with updated material.Jack M. B. Selway, Founder 2 May 2009.
