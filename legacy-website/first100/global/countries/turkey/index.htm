Rotary Club of Ankara, the First Club of Turkey

Rotary International District 2430

Also see Rotary Global History Day - Istanbul 9 October 2009
 
In a report in the British Rotary magazine 'Rotary Wheel' in August 1929, there is an account of Jim Davidson, the Special Commissioner for Rotary Extension, arriving in Istanbul, Turkey in 1928.
Although some meetings were arranged and several officials expressed interest, Davidson left Turkey with the feeling that it would take perhaps years before conditions in Turkey were such that the introduction of such a movement as Rotary could be successfully accomplished.

He was right. Istanbul was not chartered until 1956.
Strangely, Ankara became the first club to be chartered in Turkey when it was officially recognised on 23 March 1955.
There is no record of Davidson having visited Ankara.

The following extracted from the Club's web site:-

The Rotary Club of Ankara was chartered on 23 March 1955 as the first club in Turkey.
It remains the only bilingual club in Turkey where both Turkish and English are used during regular meetings.

The first official meeting was held on 24 March 1954 at the Ankara Palas. The decree number 4/4445 was issued on 11 February 1955 and entered in the RI journal on 1 April 1955 under Rotary Club number 8970.

The original Charter document was presented to the Club's committee on 7 April 1955 by the RI President at the time, Herbert Taylor in the presence also of the District 245 Governor at the time, A.J Catoni.
Indeed a memorable day for Rotary in Turkey.

These days the Club meets at the Hilton Oteli.
