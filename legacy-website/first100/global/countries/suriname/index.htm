Rotary Club of Paramaribo, the First Club of Suriname

Rotary International District 7030

 

Part of our section on South America and Central America

 
RC of Paramaribo was organized by RC Willemstad, Curacao. It was organized 2 February 1953 and chartered 21 June 1953. No Charter members alive. 

Hon. member was the last Dutch Governor/First President of Suriname. Dr. Johan Ferrier who visited our 50th Anniversary on his last trip to Suriname.  It is the oldest Club in District 7030.  Ranked Top giving club to TRF in our District in 2005. 
 
One of the so called "Old Boys" Clubs, but with rotating seating, now having 2nd Generation Members and 3rd ones entering the "League" Traditional, but fast if need for action. RAM and HIV/AIDS active. 3-H responsive and volunteering for clean water for remote villages! 
 
Open to all "Youth Initiative" with an active New Generations Highway. Helping Interact and Rotaract. 
 
With 2 sister clubs Paramaribo Central and Paramaribo Residence our Centennial project was "A First Aid Kit for all Kids" and First Aid courses to al Primary Schools together with a fire extinguisher. "Kits for Kids" enhances the issue of the importance of good first aid and being conscious of risks and remedies.

RC Paramaribo Residence
Rotary Club Paramaribo Residence is one of the three Rotary clubs in Suriname, established in June 19, 2002.
Rotary Club Paramaribo Central sponsored the initiative to establish this third club.

One of the reasons for the creation of third club was including the great need within the Surinamese community for carrying out community projects.

The oldest club in Suriname is the Rotary Club Paramaribo (founded in 1953).

The Rotary clubs are part of a global organization consisting of senior female and male executives and independent professionals and entrepreneurs, all connected by the same Rotary motto "Service Above Self".

This they do in their personal lives and in their appeal to the Rotary ideal fit.

In practice this is done by initiating and implementing community projects that relate to important areas within our community: health, education, environment, children in difficult situations.
Each Rotarian is expected that he / she is motivated to work on service projects of the community, region and even the world.
