HISTORIA de ROTARY en ESPAÑA HISTORY OF ROTARY IN SPAIN

El primer club constituido en España fue el RC Madrid con el nº 806 y cuya carta constitutiva es de 22 de octubre de 1920, siendo en ese momento el primer club de Europa Continental. Su sede era el Hotel Palas. Su primer presidente fue Florestán Aguilar (1), que luego , en 1928, fue el primer gobernador del distrito español (aunque muchos escritos se refieren a un importador de café Crótido de Simón, como primer impulsor de un club en España).

 

Quedaba España en el Distrito 60 y cuyos Gobernadores fueron: 1927-1928 FLORESTAN AGUILAR del RC Madrid 1928-1929 JOSE R. Madrid 1933-1934 JOSE GASTALVER del RC. Sevilla 1934-1935 JUAN MIGOYA del RC. Bilbao 1935-1936 FRANCISCO BASTOS del RC. Barcelona   En 1936 con la guerra civil se suspenden, las reuniones, había 31 clubes y 812 rotarios. The first club to be founded in Spain was the RC Madrid, nº 806, who received its constitutional chart on 22 October 1920, being at the time the first club in continental Europe. It met at the Hotel Palas. The first president was Mr Florestan Aquilar (1), who later, in 1928, became the first Governor of the Spanish district (Many documents, however, refer to a coffee importer, Mr Crótido de Simon as the first person to support the idea of a club in Spain.)

 

Spain became District 60 whose governors were: 1927-1928 FLORESTAN AGUILAR - RC Madrid 1928-1929 JOSE R. CARLES - RC Barcelona 1929-1930 SALVADOR ECHEANDIA GAL - RC Madrid 1930-1931 JOSE Mª ROVIRALTA - RC. Barcelona 1931-1932 MIGUEL MANTECON - RC. Zaragoza 1932-1933 DOMINGO MENDIZÁBAL - RC. Madrid 1933-1934 JOSE GASTALVER - RC. Sevilla 1934-1935 JUAN MIGOYA - RC. Bilbao 1935-1936 FRANCISCO BASTOS - RC. Barcelona   In 1936, with the civil war, the meetings are suspended. There were at the time 31 clubs and 812 rotarians. Since 1964 efforts were being made, but it was until the 18 May 1976 that the Rotary Club of Madrid received its second constitutional Chart on 6 June 1977 as club number 12616, its new seat being again the Hotel Palas.  

 

The following were Administrative Counselors of Rotary International in Spain: 1978-1979 BERNARDO GUZMAN 1979-1980 PIERRE DAVID 1980-1981 CANDIDO L. GARCIA SANJUAN 1981-1982 MEINRAD BUSSLINGUER In1982 District 220 (ESPAÑA) was created, its governors were: 1982-1983 SEBASTIAN ENSEÑAT - RC. Madrid 1983-1984 JOSE Mª ORLANDO - RC. Badajoz   From then on two districts were created: District 2200 (South of Spain and Archipelagoes) and 2210 (North of Spain). Its governors were:

 

(1) FLORESTAN AGUILAR Florestán Aguilar era un "cirujano dentista" que a finales del siglo XIX, tuvo la ocasión de atender a la Reina Maria Cristina, madre de Alfonso XIII. La historia es la siguiente: La reina regente Mª Cristina, sufrió un aparatoso acidente de carromato en la casa de Campo de Madrid. Entre sus lesiones, perdió varios dientes.  

 

Mandó llamar a su dentista, que era el Dr. Higland, un americano oriundo de Cuba, el cuál no se encontraba en ése momento en Madrid, pero habia dejado de sustituto de su consulta a un joven, el Dr. Florestán Aguilar, el cuál tuvo que atender de urgencia a la Reina.   La misma quedó muy agradecida por los servicios prestados, por lo que le nombró Dentista de la Casa Real. Las buenas relaciones que a partir de entonces tuvieron los dentistas con la casa real, hizo posible que se crease en 1901, una titulación nueva universitaria: ODONTÓLOGO, puesto que anteriormente desde 1875, se llamaban Cirujanos Dentistas, (una titulación Básica no Universitaria).   Posteriormente en los años veinte del siglo XX, D. Florestán Aguilar, y otros, fundaron el Rotary Club de España, dónde él fué el primer Gobernador.

 

(1) FLORSTAN AGUILAR Florestán Aguilar was a surgeon dentist who at the end of the XIX century had the occasion to attend to Queen Maria Cristina, mother of Alfonso XIII. The story is as follows: the regent queen Mª Cristina had a horrendous chariot accident in the Casa de Campo park of Madrid, in which she lost several teeth.

 

She asked for her dentist to be called, Dr Highland, and American born in Cuba, which was not presently in Madrid.   However, he had indicated as his substitute young doctor Florestan Aguilar who got to attend the Queen as a matter of urgency. She was very grateful for his services and named him Dentist to the Royal Household. The good relations which the dentists from then on had with the Royal house made ot possible that a new academic career was created in 1901: ODONTOLOGIST. These had been called since 1875 Surgeon Dentists, which was not a university degree.   Subsequently in the 1920's Dr Florestan Aguilar and others founded the Rotary Club of Spain, where he became the first Governor.
